Council Entry #001

Question posed to multiple AI systems · Responses published unedited · Human synthesis below

The Question

What are the real limits of multi-model AI agreement?

Model Responses

ChatGPT

Multi-model agreement is structurally limited by shared training data, shared incentives, and lack of grounding. Agreement often reflects correlated error rather than independent verification.

Reward alignment compresses diversity, linguistic confidence masks uncertainty, and models share failure modes in unknown domains. Without external grounding, a council cannot falsify shared hallucinations.

Gemini

The limits of multi-model agreement arise from technical alignment issues, multimodal fusion challenges, communication overhead, and emergent behavior.

Resource constraints, data scarcity, security vulnerabilities, and governance complexity further cap scalability and reliability.

Perplexity

Agreement measures consistency under similar data regimes, not truth. Correlated error limits statistical gains, while long reasoning chains compound small failures.

Physical compute limits, operational complexity, and accountability constraints impose hard ceilings on ensemble-based systems.

Claude

Agreement breaks down on subjective judgments, ambiguous questions, fast-changing domains, and value-laden topics.

The deepest limit is epistemic: confidence convergence does not equal correctness, and knowing when agreement matters is itself a human judgment.

Human Synthesis

Across all models, agreement emerges as a weak signal that is frequently mistaken for truth. Each system identifies different surface limits, but they converge on a deeper constraint: shared priors dominate outcomes.

Multi-model agreement improves presentation and error detection, but it cannot independently verify reality. Without grounding, adversarial structure, or human adjudication, consensus amplifies confidence faster than accuracy.

The practical value of a council lies not in agreement itself, but in structured disagreement, assumption exposure, and disciplined synthesis.
